The Professional Lifeguard Foundation

Founded in 1996

We are composed of donors/members, most of whom are still lifeguards, & are dedicated to providing scholarship awards to Los Angeles County Ocean Lifeguards. Since its inception, this non-profit organization has provided over $30,000 in unrestricted scholarship money. All Los Angeles County Ocean Lifeguards who are full-time or graduate students are eligible for consideration. Awards are based on the merits & achievements of each applicant.

The Professional Lifeguard Foundation needs You!

Please help continue to support as many scholarships as possible. Donations of all sizes are of enormous assistance to these dedicated young men & women.

Scholarship Award Recipients

2015

Top Honor – Toby Contarsy

Runner-up – Natalia Vecerek

Honorable Mention - Emily Fuller, Sebastian Garcia, Chance Pardon

Our Top Honor this year is awarded to Toby Contarsy, a rising 4th year Central Section Guard and Junior Guard instructor who transferred to UCLA from Santa Monica City College this past fall, studying Economics and English. While at SMC, Toby achieved academic excellence while competing in water polo and swimming. He achieved All-American in swimming, All-Conference in Water Polo, and was named the 2015 SMC Athlete of the Year.

Our Runner-Up for 2015 is Natalia Vecerek, a first year medical student at the Geffen School of Medicine at UCLA. Natalia, like Toby, is a rising 4th year guard. She works in the South Bay and is also a Junior Guard instructor. Natalia’s graduated from Dartmouth College in 2015, earning Magna Cum Laude and membership into the Phi Beta Kappa Honors Society. She was also Captain of the women’s swim team.

Our Honorable Mention recipients include Emily Fuller who is a Senior studying Psychology at Cal State Northridge; Sebastian Garcia, a sophomore studying Computer Science at Northwestern University; and Chance Pardon, a South Bay guard who is attending law school at UC Irvine.

2014

Top Honor – Kelsey O'Donnell

Runners-up – Derek Linsley, Charles (Charlie) Welsey Manclark, and Adam Moine

Honorable Mention - Kailey Makuta and Savannah Fletcher

Our Top Honor this year is awarded to Ms. Kelsey O'Donnell, from the Southern Section. She has competed internationally as a member on the US Surf Lifesaving Team. Her rookie year was 2008 and she is currently completing her Masters in Nursing at University of San Francisco, eventually hoping to pursue Nurse Pactitioner training.

Our Runners-Up include Derek Linsley, Charles (Charlie) Welsey Manclark, and Adam Moine.

Derek, whose rookie year was 2006 and works in Southern Section, is a graduate student at University of Hawaii in Ocean and Resources Engineering. He is planning on pursuing a Professional Engineering (PE) license, and practice in the field with aspirations of executive leadership in the long-term future.

Charlie, a Southern Section guard whose rookie year was 2013, is a senior at Boston College majoring in History with co-minors in Computer Science, and Film Studies. He has been active in Men’s Club Water Polo.

Adam lifeguards in Central Section and was a rookie in 2013. He is a sophomore at the California Maritime Academy studying Marine Transportation; his long term goal is reaching the rank of Commander as a merchant sailor. An accomplished distance swimmer, Adam has competed in many open water events, including a crossing swim from Catalina to Long Beach.

Finally, our Honorable Mention awards go to Kailey Makuta and Savannah Fletcher. Kailey is a senior at California State University, Long Beach majoring in Kinesiology. Savannah is a senior communications major at Loyola Marymount University.
